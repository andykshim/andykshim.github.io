## 2. Problem Definition

Every High Frequency Trading strategy fundamentally requires being first in line. Tick-to-trade latency is the most important metric of networking hardware in
this application, and it represents the time it takes to receive new market information from the exchange, and execute a new trade order based on the information.

The Alveo UL3422, along with the 4-port sister UL3524, are AMD's flagship cards for ultra-low latency trading. It combines the Virtex UltraScale+ XCVU2P FPGA 
with new GTF transceivers to achieve trading in nanoseconds. The UL3524 currently holds the record of 13.9 ns on the STAC-T0 benchmark measuring tick-to-trade latency [source](https://docs.stacresearch.com/news/AMD240422).

This is the card executing the trades of the biggest HFT firms today. This level of hardware is basically inaccessible to anyone but these firms, including 
acadedmic research. Many aspects of its design are a closely guarded secret. 

The goal of this project was to document the details of the card, specifically regarding bringup: near and far end loopback testing, I2C programming of the 
transceivers, etc. The doumentation is aimed to streamline future RTL development for this card, to add to AMD-provided documentation, and to document the card's 
behavior for debugging purposes.

Most of this documentation cannot be publicly disclosed. It will be made accessible for future students who sign separate NDA's with AMD.
All of the information included on this page are already publicly accesible, and will include direct links whenever possible.
